
= Kalender & Zeit-System


Harptos-Kalender mit akademischen Phasen, Jahreszeiten, Holidays, Tageszeiten, Time-Skip-Mechanik.


== Harptos-Kalender



=== Struktur


----
1 Jahr = 12 Monate × 30 Tage = 360 Tage

Monate:
  1. Hammer (Winter)
  2. Alturiak (Winter)
  3. Ches (Spring)
  4. Tarsakh (Spring)
  5. Mirtul (Spring)
  6. Kythorn (Summer)
  7. Flamerule (Summer)
  8. Eleasias (Summer)
  9. Eleint (Autumn)
  10. Marpenoth (Autumn)
  11. Uktar (Autumn)
  12. Nightal (Winter)

Jahreszeiten:
  Winter: Months 1-2, 12 (3 months)
  Spring: Months 3-5 (3 months)
  Summer: Months 6-8 (3 months)
  Autumn: Months 9-11 (3 months)
----


=== Tageszeiten


----
Per Day:
  Dawn (5:00-7:00)
  Morning (7:00-12:00)
  Afternoon (12:00-17:00)
  Evening (17:00-21:00)
  Night (21:00-5:00)

Game Default: Start session at Morning
But flexible per scene (ambush at night, dawn patrol, etc.)
----

'''


== Jahreszeiten & Effekte



=== Winter (Hammer, Alturiak, Nightal)


----
Weather: Snow common, cold always
Travel Speed: -20% (snow slows movement)
Provisions: Food scarce, prices +30% (preserved goods)
Monsters: Polar predators appear, fewer humanoids outside
Agriculture: No farming, hunting/fishing reduced
Festivals: Winter Solstice (Midwinter, month 12, day 20)

Difficulty: Hard
----


=== Frühling (Ches, Tarsakh, Mirtul)


----
Weather: Rain common, temperatures moderate
Travel Speed: Normal
Provisions: Normal prices (fields being planted)
Monsters: Normal distribution
Agriculture: Spring planting
Festivals: Spring Equinox (Greengrass, month 3, day 20)

Difficulty: Easy
----


=== Sommer (Kythorn, Flamerule, Eleasias)


----
Weather: Heat, drought possible
Travel Speed: Normal (heat -10% in desert)
Provisions: Fresh food abundant, prices -20%
Monsters: Normal, some aggressive due to heat
Agriculture: Crops growing, harvest approaching
Festivals: Summer Solstice (Midsummer, month 6, day 20)

Difficulty: Medium
----


=== Herbst (Eleint, Marpenoth, Uktar)


----
Weather: Mild, rain occasional
Travel Speed: Normal
Provisions: Harvest time, prices vary (some cheap, some abundant)
Monsters: Normal + migration patterns
Agriculture: Harvest season
Festivals: Autumn Equinox (Highharvestide, month 9, day 20)

Difficulty: Medium
----

'''


== Akademie-Phasen (wenn Akademie-based Campaign)


Akademie hat Academic Calendar separiert vom Welt-Kalender:


=== Semester-Struktur


----
Fall Semester:
  Weeks 1-4: Introductions, basic classes
  Weeks 5-9: Core curriculum
  Weeks 10-13: Midterms + practical exams
  Break: 2 weeks (Holidays)

Spring Semester:
  Weeks 1-4: New year, advanced classes
  Weeks 5-9: Project-based learning
  Weeks 10-13: Finals + thesis defense
  Break: 4 weeks (Summer break)

Summer Programs (optional):
  Weeks 1-6: Specialized training
  Weeks 7-8: Internship/practicum
----


=== Akademie-Events


----
Year 1 Induction: Month 3 (Spring, mid-month)
  → New students arrive, sorting hat ceremony, first classes

Midterm Exams: Month 6 (Summer, week 3)
  → Practical + written tests
  → Determines class advancement

Winter Festival: Month 12, days 20-25
  → Campus celebration, parties, competitions
  → Trading with visiting merchants

Spring Graduation: Month 3 (next year), day 30
  → Advanced students graduate/specialize
  → Newcomers can be promoted

Orientation Week: Month 4 (Spring, weeks 1-2)
  → New program induction
  → Classes resume full strength
----


=== Non-Akademie Characters


Non-akademie PCs are free of this calendar. They:
* Can visit akademie during breaks (social opportunity)
* Can attend parties/festivals (if invited)
* Can't participate in classes (unless became member)

'''

== Time-Skip Mechanics (wie bereits in reise-wetter.adoc)


Kurz zusammengefasst:

----
GM sagt: "3 weeks pass"

System führt automatisch aus:
  ├─ Daily weather rolls (3 weeks worth)
  ├─ Crafting projects continue + auto-rolled
  ├─ Dragon care runs at ×0.25 impact (some neglect possible)
  ├─ Provisions consumed per day
  ├─ Travel progress (if party moving)
  ├─ Item durability: NO natural decay (only use-based)
  ├─ Profession training continues
  ├─ Org reputation interactions (passive)
  └─ Events in period trigger at END of skip

Optional:
  → AI-summary of time skip
  → "For 3 weeks, you trained in the city..."
  → Brief scene descriptions
----

'''


== Downtime Tracking



=== Activities Available During Downtime


----
Per Downtime Day (non-session day):

Character can do ONE of:

1. Profession Work
   Earn X GP for that profession level

2. Crafting Project
   Work on 1 item (1 step, if recipe known)

3. Training / Learning
   Learn new skill, language, or profession
   Requires trainer NPC + gold

4. Dragon Care
   (if has dragon)
   Mandatory 2 hours, optional +2 hours activities

5. Social Activities
   Visit NPC, increase disposition
   Gain rumors, intel, quest hooks

6. Research
   Spend time in library
   Discover secrets, lore, recipe books

7. Rest / Recovery
   Heal exhaustion, meditation
   Prepare spells/rituals
----


=== App-Integration: Downtime Tracking


Mobile App allows PCs to:
* Log downtime activities between sessions
* See crafting progress
* Check dragon bond status
* Review profession earnings
* Manage reputation changes

GM gets notifications:
* "Player A finished crafting project"
* "Party dragon is hungry (-5 BP)"
* "NPC reputation updated for Player B"

'''


== Specific Time Mechanics



=== Short Rest (1 hour)


----
Character can:
  ├─ Spend 1 Hit Die to recover HP (Roll Hit Die + CON)
  ├─ Light meal + water
  ├─ Light equipment maintenance (+5-10% durability to one item)
  ├─ Meditation / prayer
  └─ Can short rest anywhere (usually)

Class Features:
  ├─ Warlocks: Refresh pact magic slots
  ├─ Monks: Refresh Ki points
  └─ Rogues: No special mechanic, just healing

Interruptions:
  Heavy combat/stress → short rest becomes invalid
  Must find safe location
----


=== Long Rest (8 hours)


----
Character must:
  ├─ Sleep 8 hours uninterrupted
  ├─ Have adequate shelter (no hostile creatures)
  ├─ Have provisions (food + water)
  └─ Not have been severely wounded

Upon completion:
  ├─ Restore HP to max
  ├─ Restore all expended hit dice
  ├─ Prepare spells (casters)
  ├─ Refresh abilities (recharge)
  └─ Gain 1 level of Exhaustion recovery (max 1/long rest)

Location-specific bonuses:
  ├─ Comfortable inn: +1d6 HP recovery (total)
  ├─ Soft bedroll in camp: Normal healing
  ├─ Sleeping on ground: Disadvantage on first CON save next day
  ├─ Sacred site (temple): +1d8 HP + spiritual boost
----


=== Exhaustion Tracking


----
Exhaustion Levels: 1-6

1 Disadvantage on ability checks
2 Speed halved
3 Disadvantage on attack rolls and saves
4 HP maximum halved
5 Speed reduced to 0
6 Death

Recovery:
  1 level per Long Rest (in safe location)
  Special features can recover more:
    Healing spell (Greater Restoration)
    Magical item with restoration
    Monk feature: spend Ki points
----

'''


== Session Planning with Calendar



=== GM Calendar Tools


----
Campaign Overview:
  Big calendar showing:
  ├─ Session dates (in real world)
  ├─ In-game time progression
  ├─ Major holidays approaching
  ├─ Crafting deadlines
  ├─ Dragon bond milestones
  └─ Org events scheduled

Planning Assistance:
  System suggests:
  "In 2 weeks, Greengrass festival arrives"
  "Crafting project finishes in 5 days"
  "Dragon's Phase-Up is imminent (3 more successful encounters needed)"
----


=== Event Calendar


----
calendar_events (Kampagne)
├── event_type (holiday|deadline|crafting_end|festival|npc_arrival|quest_deadline)
├── harptos_date (month, day)
├── description
├── auto_trigger (bool — does system handle it?)
├── notification_days_ahead (3, 7, 14, 30 days warning?)
├── affected_characters[] (who cares about this?)
└── mechanical_effect (if any)

Examples:
  Holiday "Greengrass" → Market prices -20% for food
  Crafting End → Item appears in inventory
  NPC Arrival → Quest-giver appears in tavern
  Deadline → If not completed, reputation loss
----

'''


== Multiple Calendars (if World has them)



=== Elven Calendar (Different from Harptos)


----
Some campaigns have:
  ├─ Human calendar (Harptos)
  ├─ Elven calendar (Corellon-based, longer cycles)
  ├─ Dwarven calendar (Stone-year based)
  ├─ Dragonborn calendar (Epoch-based)

Cross-reference available:
  "In Harptos: Month 3, Day 15"
  "In Elven: Starfall Cycle, 247th day"
  "In Dwarvish: Stone-Year 1,247, Season 3"

But campaign typically uses ONE primary calendar
Others are just for flavor
----

'''


== DB-Struktur


----
harptos_calendar (Welt)
├── year (current campaign year, e.g., 1372)
├── month (1-12)
├── day (1-30)
├── dayofweek (Monday-Sunday, derived)
├── season (winter|spring|summer|autumn)
└── current_time_of_day (dawn|morning|afternoon|evening|night)

calendar_events (Kampagne)
├── id (PK)
├── event_type
├── harptos_month
├── harptos_day
├── description
├── is_recurring (bool)
├── affected_characters[] (jsonb)
├── mechanical_effects (jsonb)
├── notification_sent (bool)
└── notification_dates (jsonb: [{date, sent_at}])

session_logs (Kampagne)
├── id (PK)
├── date_real_world (YYYY-MM-DD)
├── date_in_game_start (harptos date)
├── date_in_game_end (harptos date, after time skip)
├── time_elapsed_days
├── scenes[] (scene_ids)
├── downtime_activities[] (jsonb per character)
└── notes (recap, major events)

downtime_log (Kampagne)
├── character_id (FK)
├── activity_type (profession|crafting|training|dragon|social|research|rest)
├── date_start (harptos)
├── date_end (harptos)
├── gold_earned (if profession work)
├── craft_step_completed (if crafting)
├── relationship_change (if social)
└── result_description
----


== Session Pacing with Calendar



=== Recommended Session Timeline


----
Session 1: Month 1, days 1-5 (introduction, first encounter)
Session 2: Month 1, days 5-8 (dungeon delve)
Session 3: Month 1, days 8-12 (travel + social)
Session 4: Month 1, days 12-15 (combat challenge)
Session 5: Month 1, days 15-20 (climax, downtime planning)
[DOWNTIME: 10 days, Month 1, days 20-30]
Session 6: Month 2, days 1-5 (new arc begins)
...
[HOLIDAY: Greengrass, Month 3, day 20]
[MAJOR EVENT: Dragon Phase-Up anticipated]
----

This gives players sense of progression and allows GM to plan accordingly.
