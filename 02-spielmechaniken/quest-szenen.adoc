
= Quest & Szenen-System


Acht Quest-Typen mit Story-Hooks, sechs Szenen-Typen, strukturierte Übergänge.


== Quest-System



=== Acht Quest-Typen



[cols="1,1,1,1",options="header"]
|===
| Quest-Typ | Struktur | Beispiel | Reward

| Fetch | Bringe Item von A zu B | Deliver potion to healer | Gold + Disposition
| Kill | Defeat specific creature | Slay the corrupted wolf | Gold + renown
| Discover | Find location or info | Locate the hidden temple | Info + loot
| Protect | Defend person/place | Guard caravan overnight | Gold + faction rep
| Rescue | Save someone | Free captured merchant | Gold + personal quest
| Gather | Collect multiple items | Gather herbs for potion | Gold + crafting mats
| Infiltrate | Sneak into location | Spy on bandit camp | Gold + info
| Custom | GM-defined | Anything else | Variable
|===



=== Quest-Struktur


----
quests (Welt)
├── id (PK)
├── name
├── giver_npc_id (FK)
├── quest_type (enum: 8 types above)
├── description (narrative)
├── objectives[] (jsonb)
│   ├── {objective_text, type, target, completed: bool}
├── reward_gold
├── reward_items[] (potential loot)
├── reward_xp
├── reward_reputation (faction_id, amount)
├── pre_requirements (level, traits, quest chain)
├── success_condition (all|any|specific_count)
└── failure_condition (time limit? person dies? etc.)
----


=== Objectives (strukturiert)


Jedes Quest hat 1-5 Objectives:

----
Objective 1: "Travel to Blackwood Forest"
  Type: LOCATION
  Target: blackwood_forest_id
  Completed when: Player arrives

Objective 2: "Find the Shrine of Moonlight"
  Type: DISCOVER
  Target: shrine_location_id
  Hint available: If requested, "In the eastern glen"

Objective 3: "Collect 5 moonflowers"
  Type: GATHER
  Target: item_moonflower (need 5 quantity)
  Completed when: Inventory contains ≥5

Objective 4: "Return to Elara"
  Type: DELIVER
  Target: npc_elara_id
  Completed when: At same location
----


=== Quest Rewards


----
Reward Gold: 50 GP (variable by difficulty)
Reward Items: Loot table (random or fixed)
Reward XP: 100-500 XP (party divided)
Reward Reputation: "Farmer's Guild" +10
Reward Disposition: Elara's disposition +20
Reward Faction Membership: Automatically joined "Farmer's Guild"
Reward Quest Hook: Next quest unlocked
----


=== Quest Chain (Sequential)


----
Quest 1: "Investigate bandits"
  Reward: → Unlocks Quest 2

Quest 2: "Gather evidence"
  Reward: → Unlocks Quest 3

Quest 3: "Confront bandit leader"
  Success: → Complete chain, faction reputation +50
  Failure: → Alternative Quest 3b: "Flee and rebuild"
----

'''


== Szenen-Typen



=== Sechs Szenen-Kategorien



[cols="1,1,1,1",options="header"]
|===
| Szene-Typ | Struktur | Beispiel | Resolution

| Social | Conversation-based | Persuading merchant | Disposition +/-, info gained
| Combat | Fight-based | Battle with bandits | Loot, HP changes
| Discovery | Exploration-based | Finding hidden room | New map area, loot
| Chase | Movement-based | Fleeing from guards | Success/failure on Athletics
| Moral | Decision-based | Save ally or village? | Consequence cascade
| Environmental | Obstacle-based | Bridge collapsed, need cross | Various solutions
|===



=== Szenen-Struktur


----
scenes (Kampagne)
├── id (PK)
├── session_id (FK)
├── scene_type (enum: 6 types)
├── title
├── description (mood, NPCs, setting)
├── objectives[] (what needs to happen)
├── reveal_elements (jsonb)
│   ├── {condition, element_description}
│   └── "If PC searches room: Find diary with clue"
├── complications[] (optional twists)
├── resolution_type (enum: combat_victory|negotiated|avoided|custom)
└── transition_to_scene_id (next scene)
----


=== Reveal Elements (Conditional Info)


----
Reveal Elements sind versteckte Info die sich offenbaren wenn:
  a) Spieler sucht aktiv (Search-Action)
  b) Ein Skilcheck erfolgt (Natural 20)
  c) Ein Trait-relevant Moment passiert
  d) Dracheninstinkt triggert (Dragon senses danger)
----

'''


== Szenische Übergänge


Szenen fließen nicht abrupt ineinander. Übergänge haben _dramatische Struktur_:


=== Übergangs-Struktur


----
Scene A ends (e.g., Combat resolved)
  ↓ [Transition Phase]
Brief pause. "The fight is over. What do you do?"
  ↓ [Decision Point]
Players choose next action or GM offers directions
  ↓ [Transition Narration]
"You rest for an hour. As night falls, you hear hoofbeats..."
  ↓
Scene B begins (e.g., Mysterious Riders approach)
----


=== Automatische Übergänge (based on Outcome)


----
Combat Victory:
  → Loot Phase (5 min)
  → Rest / Recovery (if needed)
  → Travel or Encounter next location

Negotiated Success:
  → Dialog conclusion / Disposition update
  → NPC provides information / quest hook
  → Party decides next move

Failure / Defeat:
  → Consequences (captured? fled? death?)
  → New Scene based on failure
  → Alternative path opens
----

'''


== Session-Workflow mit Szenen



=== Standard Session Structure


----
[0-10 min] Opening Recap
  GM: "Last session you defeated the bandits..."
  System optionally: AI-generated recap from previous session-log

[10-120 min] Active Scenes
  Typically 2-4 scenes per 3-hour session
  Each scene 20-40 minutes depending on complexity

[5 min] End-of-Session Bookkeeping
  - XP awarded
  - Treasure distributed
  - Traits evaluated for evolution
  - Decision-logging (if story-important choices made)

[Optional] AI Session Recap
  Craig-Pipeline: Audio → Whisper/Gemini Transkript → AI Zusammenfassung
  GM reviews + approves
  Published in session-log
----


=== Optional: AI Recap Generation


----
System Requirements:
  - Session was audio-recorded (optional)
  - Whisper/Gemini transcription (if audio)
  - Campaign database access

Process:
  1. Audio → Transcript
  2. AI extracts key moments:
     ├─ Major decisions made
     ├─ Trait-relevant triggers
     ├─ Combat results
     ├─ NPC interactions
     ├─ New information
     └─ Hooks for next session

  3. AI generates summary (~500 words)
  4. GM reviews + edits
  5. Published in campaign-log + sent to players

Features:
  - Automatic journal entries suggested
  - Trait-evolution flagged
  - Decision-logging pre-filled
  - "Next time on Flamenritter..." teaser optional
----

'''


== Loot-Verteilung (nach Kampf)



=== Kampf-Loot-Workflow


----
SCHRITT 1: Kampf-Ende
  Spieler haben Gegner besiegt
  Combat-Fenster zeigt: "Kampf beendet. Feinde fallen."

SCHRITT 2: Automatischer Loot-Vorschlag
  System generiert Loot:
  - Creature Drop Table (basierend auf CR)
  - Random Rarity/Quality Items
  - Gold (basierend auf Creature Wealth)

  Beispiel:
  "2 Banditen (CR 1/8) each drop:
    - 15 GP
    - Leather armor (Common Q2)
    - (30% chance) Dagger (Common Q1)"

SCHRITT 3: GM Review
  GM sieht Vorschlag
  Options:
    a) Accept loot as-is
    b) Remove specific items (didn't make sense)
    c) Add items (creature had special treasure)
    d) Replace with custom items

SCHRITT 4: Spieler sehen Loot
  "You see: 30 GP, 2 leather armors, 1 dagger"

SCHRITT 5: Spieler-Discussion
  Spieler discutieren in-character:
  "I get the gold, you take the armor?"
  Oder: "Let me roll for the dagger"

SCHRITT 6: Loot-Verteilung
  GM assigns items (Dropdown oder Drag-Drop)
  System moves items to inventories
  Gold shared or manually divided

SCHRITT 7: Confirmation
  GM: "Loot distributed. What's next?"
  Alles geloggt in combat_loot_logs
----


=== Loot-Tables


----
random_encounter_loot (Welt)
├── creature_id (FK) oder cr_rating
├── drop_chance (0-100%)
├── rarity_ranges (jsonb)
│   ├── {rarity, drop_chance%, quantity_range}
├── gold_amount (dice formula)
├── unique_items[] (special loot)
└── quest_item_chance (% it contains relevant quest item)
----

'''


== Decision-Logging



=== Wenn Entscheidung wichtig ist


GM kann Decision loggen:

----
decision_logs (Kampagne)
├── id (PK)
├── session_id (FK)
├── scene_id (FK)
├── decision_text ("Save the village or pursue bandit leader?")
├── decision_type (moral|tactical|roleplay|consequence)
├── severity (1-5: 1=minor, 5=campaign-altering)
├── who_decided (party_vote|single_character|group_consensus)
├── chosen_option ("Save the village")
├── consequences (jsonb)
│   ├── { system: quest|disposition|reputation|bounty|criminal|trait_shift|event,
│   │    reference_id, description }
└── follow_up_decision_id (wenn diese Entscheidung später Konsequenzen hat)
----


=== Beispiel-Decision-Konsequenz-Kette


----
DECISION 1: "Do you save the captured merchant or protect the caravan?"
  Choice: "Protect caravan"
  Consequence: Merchant dies (NPC death) → Disposition -50 with Merchant's Guild

FOLLOW-UP: Merchant's Guild contacts party, DECISION 2 created:
DECISION 2: "Will you atone by hunting the bandits who killed him?"
  Choice: "Yes"
  Consequence: Quest "Avenge the merchant" created, Reputation +20 with Guild

FOLLOW-UP: Success on DECISION 2's quest, DECISION 3:
DECISION 3: "Guild offers position - accept membership?"
  Choice: "Accept"
  Consequence: Official Guild Member, passive income, reputation locked at +80
----

'''
